Total usage distribution
This chart shows how the total amount of Watts usage varies in time for the selected timeframe. You can edit the timeframe via the button in the top right. A Watt is a unit of power, which means the rate at which energy is consumed/produced. For example: a device of 100 Watt will consume energy faster than a 50 Watt device. You can interpret the area under this curve as the actual amount of consumed energy.
Usage distribution per circuit
This chart shows for each selected circuit how the energy usage in kWhs for varies in time. A darker square means more energy was used in that period. The selected timeframe is divided into smaller ones for which the total energy usage per circuit is calculated. You can edit the timeframe via the button in the top right. You can edit which circuits are shown below the chart. A Watt hour (Wh) is a unit of energy, which tells us about the amount of work that has been done by some devices. A kWh or kilo Watt hour is just 1000 Watt hours. Watt hours refer to enegy used / work done for some time period. For example: a 60 Watt lightbulb that is on for 1 hour will consume 1 Wh of energy.
